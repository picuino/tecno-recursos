4. Double acting cylinder¶

A double-acting cylinder is a cylinder that has two air inlet and outlet paths. The stem can come out forcefully when air is injected into the rear and can enter forcefully when air is injected into the front.

The output force of the stem is always a little greater than the input force because the compressed air cannot put pressure on the area occupied by the stem.

Double-acting cylinders are controlled by a 4/2 valve or by a 5/2 valve. Both valves are similar and only differ in the number of exhausts they have (one exhaust for the 4/2 valve and two exhausts for the 5/2 valve).

When we activate the 5/2 valve, the air coming from the maintenance unit passes to the upper left valve passage and enters the rear part of the cylinder. As a result, the cylinder fills with air at the rear while air at the front escapes to the atmosphere. The result is that the cylinder rod comes outwards, pushing the load in front of it.
